The place

When the moor goes dark, the wok goes on.

Choys has kept the corner of Town Hall Place glowing for years — the little Chinese takeaway that Bovey Tracey walks down to when the light drops behind Haytor and nobody fancies cooking.

Everything is made to order the moment you call: crispy aromatic duck carved with pancakes, king prawns fired in black bean and garlic, curries built from a sauce that's been simmering since teatime — mild through to properly hot, cooked how you order it. No heat lamps, no shortcuts — just a proper evening supper, wrapped warm to carry home.

— The Choy family
